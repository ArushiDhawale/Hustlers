Is This Normal?

If you’ve ever looked at your period and wondered “Is this normal?” — you’re not alone. Menstrual blood can vary in color, flow, and duration from person to person, and even from cycle to cycle.

Most of these changes are completely normal. Understanding what’s typical can help you feel more confident about your body — and know when it’s time to seek medical advice.

Period Blood Color Guide

Menstrual blood doesn’t always look the same, and that’s okay.

Bright Red

Common during the first few days. usually means a healthy, steady flow.

Dark Red or Brown

Often seen towards the end. This is older blood that took longer to leave the uterus — completely normal.

Pink

Can happen when blood mixes with cervical fluid. Often appears during lighter flow days.

Gray / Unusual

**This is not normal** and could indicate an infection. It’s best to consult a doctor immediately.

Blood Clots: Should You Worry?

Small Clots (Coin-sized)

Common during heavy flow days and usually harmless.

Large or Frequent Clots

If clots are large, painful, or happen often, it may be a sign of hormonal imbalance. A consultation is recommended.

Duration & Flow

  • Typical duration: 3 to 7 days.
  • Flow usually heavier in first few days.
  • Seek advice if soaking pads every 1-2 hours.

When Should You See a Doctor?

It’s okay to ask for help. Doctors hear these concerns every day. No embarrassment needed.

Severe or worsening pain
Extremely heavy bleeding
Periods stop for months without pregnancy
Strong odor or unusual discharge
Symptoms that feel “not normal for you”

Your comfort and health matter — there’s no shame in taking care of yourself.

A Gentle Reminder

Every body is different. What’s normal for someone else may not be normal for you — and that’s okay. Paying attention to patterns over time is more important than comparing yourself to others. Luna is here to help you track changes and feel confident.
